    • About This Tool

    This tool is to help visually impaired students/individuals learn mathematic concepts.

    Who is the target audience?

    Students who can speak English, and who use screen readers. The tool might also be useful for teachers/parents of those students. The tool may also be used to teach very young children the basics of mathematics.


    The tool is focussed on topics that can be displayed as a table that a screen-reader, like JAWS, NVDA and Windows Narrator, can read. Topics that will be covered include simple addition, subtraction, multiplication and division. Also included will be topics that will be useful for high-school students, like synthetic division of first and second order, polynomial division, matrices, decimal to binary conversion and viceversa, ....


    The tool will have buttons like "Solve", "Step-By-Step", "Exercise" and "Verify". "Solve" will just solve the problem. "Step-By-Step" will explain the steps as it is being solved. "Exercise" is for the student to try on their own and "Verify" is to check their work. There is also a "About" section that gives a brief description of the topic, and "Steps" section that shows the steps as the problem is being solved. The final result will appear under "Answer".


    If you want to start over a topic, press F5 to refresh the screen.
